Why this exists

Nobody around you can give you the complete read.

What a delivered punch list looks like

Genuinely strong, lead with these

  • Your retention curve for the first 40 users is the best slide in the deck and it's currently slide 14. Move it to slide 3.
  • Founder-market fit is real here. You lived this problem for 6 years at ██████. Say that in the first minute, not in the team slide.
  • The pricing experiment you ran in March is stronger evidence than the TAM slide. Investors see invented TAMs daily. They rarely see real willingness-to-pay data.

Where to focus before you pitch

  • Your acquisition story assumes a channel that dies at scale. You need an answer for what happens after the first 200 customers from ████████.
  • The "AI-powered" framing is working against you. Three competitors say the same words. Your actual differentiator is the workflow position, not the model.
  • Unit economics slide mixes one-time and recurring revenue. Any partner will catch it in 30 seconds. Separate them before anyone sees this.

Concrete tips

  • Build vs buy: stop building your own ██████████ pipeline. Two vendors do it better and your defensibility isn't there anyway.
  • Rehearse the "why hasn't ██████ built this" answer out loud. Your current version takes 90 seconds. It needs to take 15.

Why is payment up front?
Two reasons. Slots are scarce because I'm a full time CTO, and prepayment is what keeps the calendar real. And the session only works if you've decided to hear the full read. Paying is that decision. You get the punch list no matter what we find.
Is my idea safe with you?
Yes. Everything you share stays between us, and I'm happy to sign your NDA before the call. I'm a full time CTO with my own products. I'm not in the business of ideas, I'm in the business of judgment.
What if I don't have a deck yet?
Even better. The earlier we talk, the cheaper the fixes. A session at the idea stage regularly saves founders months of building the wrong thing. Bring a memo, a Loom, or just your head.
Will you tell me my idea is bad?
If parts of it are, yes, clearly and with reasons. But every session covers what's strong with the same rigor, and founders are usually surprised by how much is. You leave knowing where you stand, which beats leaving flattered.
Can you introduce me to investors?
No, and be careful with anyone who sells that. What I offer is the read that makes your existing chances count. Your intros are yours. My job is making sure you don't spend them on the weak version of your pitch.
Who is this not for?
Founders looking for validation rather than a read, agencies pitching client work, and anyone post Series A with a full advisory bench already. You'd be paying for something you have.
