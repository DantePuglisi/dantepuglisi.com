Why I do this

It started with a favor.

A friend asked me to look at his friend's pitch, and 90 minutes of honest back and forth changed how that founder saw his own company.

Not because I'm smarter than him. My day job at YRW Tech is buying forgotten apps, growing them, and selling them, so I judge ideas, products, and execution for a living. And I had no reason to tell him anything except what I actually saw.

That's what a session is. All of that experience, on your side of the table, looking at exactly one thing: your startup. Best before you've launched anywhere, before any investor has seen your name, or days before the meeting that matters.

To be clear, AI might be the best thing that ever happened to founders. Products that took a year now ship in weeks, and AI even helped me write this page, it lets me say things better than I would alone. But it has one limitation built in: it's on your side. It will polish whatever you bring it, and a pitch that has only ever been polished has never been tested.

Questions founders ask

The honest FAQ.

Why is payment up front?
Two reasons. Slots are scarce because I'm a full time CTO, and prepayment is what keeps the calendar real. And the session only works if you've decided to hear everything, the good and the bad. Paying is that decision.
Is my idea safe with you?
Yes. Everything you share stays between us, and I'm happy to sign your NDA before the call.
What if I don't have a deck yet?
That works too. We spend the time on the idea and the business model instead of the slides. Plenty of founders book before anything is built, and the session is just as useful. Bring whatever you have, even if it's only what's in your head.
Will you tell me my idea is bad?
If parts of it are, yes, clearly and with reasons. But every session covers what's strong with the same rigor, and founders are usually surprised by how much is. You leave knowing where you stand, which beats leaving flattered.
My investor meeting is in two days. Too late?
That's often the best moment. If you just made big changes to the deck or the story, fresh outside feedback before the meeting is exactly what this is for. If the calendar doesn't show anything soon enough, message me and I'll do my best to make same day or next day work.
Is this a one time thing?
Up to you. Many founders come back around milestones: a new version of the pitch, a pivot, an investor process heating up. Returning founders book at preferred rates. The first session is the expensive one, on purpose. I'd rather build a long relationship with the founders I advise than charge strangers full price twice.
Who is this not for?
Founders looking for validation rather than honest feedback, agencies pitching client work, and anyone post Series A with a full advisory bench already.
